Process improvement in surgery often stops at documentation. We believe in systematic redesign.

## The Limitation of Traditional Approaches

Most workflow optimization efforts:
- Map current processes
- Identify obvious inefficiencies
- Make incremental adjustments

This captures low-hanging fruit but misses fundamental redesign opportunities.

## Engineering vs. Documenting

Workflow engineering is different. It asks:
- What constraints are actually necessary?
- Which handoffs could be eliminated?
- How should information flow?
- What coordination mechanisms optimize outcomes?

## Case Study: Preoperative Huddles

Traditional approach: "Let's have a huddle before each case"

Engineering approach:
- What information needs to be synchronized?
- When must each party know it?
- What decisions can be pre-made?
- How do we verify readiness without meetings?

Result: Redesigned information flow that eliminated 70% of huddles while improving safety checklist compliance.

## Systems Thinking

Individual process improvements plateau quickly. System-level redesign compounds.

We don't optimize surgical scheduling in isolation. We engineer the entire perioperative system:
- Scheduling ↔ Resource allocation
- Patient flow ↔ Capacity planning
- Staff coordination ↔ Information architecture
- Quality metrics ↔ Operational decisions

Complex, yes. But that's where transformational gains live.
