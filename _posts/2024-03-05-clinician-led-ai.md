AI tools for surgery are proliferating. Most miss the mark because they're not built by people who work in ORs.

## The Problem with Outside-In Design

When technologists build surgical tools without clinical immersion:
- They optimize metrics that don't matter
- They add friction to existing workflows
- They solve problems that don't exist
- They miss problems that do

## What Clinical Leadership Brings

**Workflow Literacy**  
Understanding how ORs actually function, not how they theoretically should.

**Metric Grounding**  
Knowing which measurements drive outcomes vs. which are vanity KPIs.

**Adoption Realism**  
Designing for real-world constraints: time pressure, cognitive load, institutional inertia.

**Safety Primacy**  
Baking in clinical risk awareness that pure data scientists lack.

## Example: Scheduling Optimization

Pure algorithmic approach:
- Maximize OR utilization
- Minimize idle time
- Optimize revenue per block

Clinical-led approach:
- Ensure appropriate case sequencing
- Build in realistic turnover buffers
- Account for surgeon fatigue factors
- Prioritize patient safety over utilization

Both use advanced analytics. One creates dangerous efficiency. One creates sustainable optimization.
